# Paper I full rewrite memo (v1)

## What changed

This is a full editorial rewrite of Paper I, not a phrase-level scrub. The new version is built around four principles:

1. **RA-native mechanism first.** The paper now states DAG growth, BDG filtering, actualization, LLC, kernel locality, and the d=4 arithmetic spine as the active proof chain.
2. **Causal Set Theory context made explicit.** The introduction and core sections now situate RA in the immediate CST lineage while explaining what RA adds: primitive actualization, the BDG acceptance kernel as an event filter, structured potentia, and the LLC.
3. **Compare without borrowing.** The paper now explicitly compares RA to QM, GR, and CST throughout, but does not let those frameworks define the ontology or proof path.
4. **Open issues stated plainly.** The rewrite now foregrounds three major open targets: sparse-regime frequency laws adequate to the quantum domain, analytic closure of D4U02, and native macroscopic kinematic / geometric closure.

## Major section-level revisions

### Front matter and abstract
- Rewritten to introduce RA as a CST-descended but distinct theory.
- Explicitly states that similarities to QM/GR are comparison points, not derivational targets.
- Explicitly states what is still open.

### Introduction
- Rebuilt from scratch.
- Presents RA as a different starting point for physics rather than as a development diary.
- Explains CST lineage and RA's additions.
- States from the start that the paper has both a native and comparative job.

### Causal graph / BDG / sequential growth sections
- Repositioned to explain how RA both inherits from and departs from CST.
- Emphasizes that the BDG action is used locally as an acceptance kernel, not only as a continuum-recovery device.
- Makes the actualization ontology and bimodal potentia/actuality structure more explicit.

### Actualization / measurement sections
- Reframed around the contrast with QM.
- Explicitly states why RA does not need a primitive Born rule in the same way standard QM does.
- Explicitly states that deriving the observed quantum frequency structure remains open.

### Finite propagation / d=4 sections
- Reframed around the contrast with GR and continuum kinematics.
- Makes clear that RA targets many of the same observables by a different route.
- Separates compiled arithmetic support from still-open geometric closure.

### Status summary and conclusion
- Rebuilt to distinguish active native support, comparative cartography, and open native targets.
- Conclusion now presents RA as a CST-rooted alternative starting point, not a denial of current physics.
