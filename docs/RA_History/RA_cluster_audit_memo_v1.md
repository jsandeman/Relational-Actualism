# RA Cluster Audit Memo v1

## Main findings

### Paper III

- The GR bridge is the right next flagship cluster, but it remains a distributed derivation. Its cleanest discrete support is LLC + Graph Cut; its weakest points are the AQFT adapter assumptions and the lack of a single suite-primary weak-field benchmark derivation.

- Rotation-curve and Bullet-Cluster scripts are valuable because they are honest: the former is calibrated, the latter explicitly says the framework is consistent but not yet proved.


### Paper II

- Paper II has the strongest default-root formal bedrock outside Paper I: topology closure, confinement depths, and exact Koide.

- Its biggest audit need is framing discipline: the formal core is stronger than the prose sometimes makes visible, while the mass/gauge bridges are weaker than their rhetoric sometimes suggests.


### Paper IV

- Paper IV is useful as an extension paper, but it should not yet carry the main burden of showing RA is a calculationally useful physical theory.

- The immediate blockers are suite primacy (RACI/RAQI/RAQM dependencies), theorem-location blur for Markov blanket shielding, the KCB formula mismatch, and the prototype state of the assembly pipeline.


## Strategic implication

For the 'RA is viable and useful' agenda, the cleanest public benchmark ladder is still:

1. Casimir
2. weak-field light deflection/lensing
3. Mercury perihelion
4. rotation curves / Bullet Cluster
5. KCB / spin-bath


Paper IV can remain in the suite, but it should follow the core physical benchmark programme rather than lead it.
