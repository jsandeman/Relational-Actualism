
# Audit of Papers II–IV Under the Corrected Framing Rule
**Date:** April 23, 2026

This audit uses the corrected framing rule:

- RA should be stated natively first.
- Existing theories should be compared explicitly for orientation and contrast.
- Existing theories are not the proof path or mechanism.
- It is good practice to say plainly where RA has no analogue of a standard construct.

Under that rule, Papers II–IV are in significantly better shape than they would appear under a harsher “Nature only, no benchmark language” reading.

---

## Overall verdict

All three papers are broadly aligned with the corrected rule already.

They do **not** need a doctrine purge.
They need only **targeted refinement**:
- preserve the comparisons,
- sharpen the compare/contrast structure,
- and keep proof-path language strictly RA-native.

---

# Paper II — Matter, Forces, and Renewal Motifs

## Overall judgment
Paper II is conceptually well aligned with the corrected rule and is probably the strongest of the three on framing discipline.

It does three things especially well:
1. states the matter programme natively from DAG + BDG + LLC + motif renewal,
2. keeps the Standard Model explicitly in view as the current organization of the same empirical domain,
3. repeatedly says where RA's mechanism is different and where RA has no direct analogue.  

That is exactly the right editorial stance.

## What already works well

### 1. The introduction is now on the right side of the line
Paper II says:
- it is not written as if the Standard Model did not exist,
- the Standard Model defines the empirical domain RA must address,
- RA does not borrow its mechanism,
- RA may have no direct analogue of some standard constructs.

That is close to ideal for exposition.

### 2. The CST lineage is stated properly
Paper II openly places itself in the immediate lineage of causal set theory and then names the additional RA ingredients that turn that substrate into a matter programme.

### 3. The compare-and-contrast paragraphs are doing useful work
The paper repeatedly uses the pattern:
- state RA-native claim,
- name Nature-facing target,
- compare with Standard Model mechanism,
- state open target.

That is excellent structure.

### 4. “No analogue” usage is strong
Paper II correctly says that in some places RA has no direct analogue of the standard-theory construct. That should remain.

## Remaining issues

### A. One sentence is still slightly too strong in legacy-benchmark direction
“The Standard Model's successes define an empirical domain RA must address” is workable, but can be improved.

Better:
- “Nature presents masses, lifetimes, interaction ranges, and asymmetries; the Standard Model is the dominant current organization of that domain.”

This is a subtle refinement, not a philosophical reversal.

### B. Be careful with any lingering inherited language that sounds like RA is replacing QCD term-for-term
Paper II is mostly clean here, but when it contrasts itself with QCD or Standard Model confinement language, it should keep saying:
- same observable domain,
- different mechanism,
rather than sounding like RA owes a one-to-one substitute for every standard concept.

### C. Numerical overlays must remain clearly downstream
Paper I already set this discipline. Paper II should keep it firm whenever measured-number targets appear.

## Bottom line for Paper II
Paper II is **already broadly aligned** with the corrected framing rule.
It needs only a **light wording pass**, not restructuring.

# Paper III — Gravity, Cosmology, Severance, and Complexity

## Overall judgment
Paper III is also substantially aligned with the corrected rule, but it is the paper most likely to drift into benchmark-language if not watched carefully.

That is not because it is badly written. It is because the gravitational/cosmological domain is so heavily dominated by GR and ΛCDM language that comparisons can easily start sounding like obligations.

## What already works well

### 1. The opening stance is good
Paper III says:
- it is not written as though GR and ΛCDM did not exist,
- those theories organize the empirical domain RA must confront,
- they do not supply RA's mechanism.

That is exactly the right compare/contrast formula.

### 2. The CST positioning is excellent
The abstract and introduction make clear that Paper III is in continuity with CST but not just a restatement of CST.

### 3. Causal severance is framed correctly
This is the strongest conceptual section in the paper.
It is stated not as “RA’s version of singularity resolution,” but as a finite causal mechanism with no close analogue in standard frameworks.

That is excellent and should be preserved.

### 4. Boundary-law and entropy discussion is well handled
Paper III does a good job saying:
- same observational domain as black-hole entropy and horizons,
- different primitive object,
- different route,
- coefficient-level closure still open.

## Remaining issues

### A. Watch any sentence that sounds like “GR is the thing to recover”
Most of the paper avoids this, but gravitational language is sticky.
Wherever the prose says things like:
- “compare with gravitational and cosmological observables,”
that is fine.
Wherever it might start sounding like:
- “recover GR” or “bridge to GR,”
that should be resisted.

### B. Keep ΛCDM comparison observational, not ontological
Paper III is already mostly careful here.
The safest pattern is:
- “ΛCDM organizes the observations through vacuum energy and dark sectors; RA asks whether some of the same observations arise from sparse-regime topology, inherited source history, and inhomogeneous fit effects.”

### C. The severance claims should remain the clearest “no analogue” statements in the suite
This is a strength, not a risk.
Emphasize it.

## Bottom line for Paper III
Paper III is **conceptually strong and largely aligned already**.
It needs a **moderate wording pass** to ensure that GR / ΛCDM remain comparison classes, not implicit targets.

# Paper IV — Complexity, Life, and the Causal Firewall

## Overall judgment
Paper IV is better aligned with the corrected rule than I had previously given it credit for.

Its introduction and abstract already do exactly what they should:
- compare with existing theories and domains,
- keep RA native,
- say where RA does not take standard constructs as primitive,
- keep open targets explicit.

## What already works well

### 1. The abstract is very good
It says comparison is explicit but secondary, names CST as lineage, and explains what RA adds that makes a complexity programme possible.

### 2. The introduction is mature
Paper IV explicitly says:
- existing theories are not irrelevant,
- RA is addressing the same empirical domain by a different mathematical route,
- Assembly Theory, systems biology, and decoherence can be compared usefully,
- RA does not take decoherence or a Born rule as primitive.

That is exactly the corrected rule in practice.

### 3. The compare-and-contrast structure is strong
Paper IV’s best feature is that it does not pretend that biology / consciousness can be made intelligible without comparison to existing frameworks.
It uses those frameworks as reader orientation while keeping RA’s mechanism native.

### 4. Strong “no analogue / not primitive” usage
Paper IV is especially good at saying:
- RA does not begin with decoherence as primitive,
- RA does not take Born-rule statistics as primitive,
- RA does not reduce boundaries to merely statistical partitions.

That is valuable and should stay.

## Remaining issues

### A. The legacy DFT / RACI dependency still needs to stay softened
This remains the main live editorial issue from earlier audit work.
The safest immediate stance is still:
- keep DFT-style support as an open computational target unless migrated directly into the suite.

### B. Do not let Markov-blanket language quietly become primary support
Paper IV seems to be handling this better now, but it remains a place to watch.

### C. The consciousness section should keep its epistemic caution
It is already flagged as the most exploratory part of the most exploratory paper.
That discipline should remain exactly as it is.

## Bottom line for Paper IV
Paper IV is **already quite well aligned** with the corrected rule.
Its main remaining issue is not framing doctrine but support-surface discipline.
