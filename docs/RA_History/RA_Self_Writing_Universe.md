## 1. The question

Can any humanly devisable model of Nature capture not just the mere
fact that something exists, but the mere fact that it moves?

---

## 2. What no model can do

No model can explain why anything happens. A model describes structure.
Happening is not a structure — it is what structure is the structure
*of*. Whitehead called this "creativity" and declared it the ultimate
category: the one thing that cannot be derived from anything else.

Standard physics disguises this limitation by embedding happening in
its formalism as a parameter. The Schrödinger equation says
iℏ ∂ψ/∂t = Hψ, and the variable t carries the happening. But nobody
explains why the equation executes. Why does the Hamiltonian generate
evolution? Because it does. That is a brute fact hidden under
mathematical notation.

---

## 3. What RA does differently

RA does not explain happening. It makes happening primitive.

Kernel Axiom B says: actualization is irreversible. The graph grows.
That statement is not derived from anything. It is the starting point.
Everything else — spacetime, particles, forces, complexity, time
itself — is the *pattern* of that growing.

The "mere fact that it moves" is the one thing RA refuses to explain,
because it is the one thing RA uses as its foundation.

## 4. Time and energy as two sides of the same coin

In RA, this is not a metaphor. It is literally true.

**Time** = the fact that actualization events accumulate (the graph grows).

**Energy** = the rate at which they accumulate (how many events per
external reference interval).

The Compton time τ = ℏ/mc² makes this identity explicit: a particle
with mass m has energy mc², and its internal clock ticks at rate m/ℏ.
More mass = more energy = faster internal clock = more actualization
events per unit of graph growth elsewhere.

Energy IS actualization rate. Not metaphorically. Definitionally.

This is why today's lifetime computations worked: the W boson lives
for ~30 internal steps and the ρ lives for ~4.5 internal steps. These
are dimensionless facts about the graph. They become facts about
seconds only when multiplied by the Compton time — which is itself
just the conversion factor between the graph's intrinsic tempo and
human units.

## 6. The closed loop

The deepest structural feature of RA is that the dynamics is a
completely closed loop with no external input:

    The graph grows
    → new vertices are proposed
    → the BDG score filters them (S_BDG > 0?)
    → accepted vertices are inscribed
    → inscribed vertices change the local density μ
    → the local density determines Poisson rates λ_k = μ^k/k!
    → the rates determine what gets proposed next
    → the graph grows

There is no external clock driving the evolution. No external energy
source powering the process. No Hamiltonian that needs someone to
"turn on." The graph writes itself according to rules that are
themselves consequences of the graph's own geometry.

**The universe is not a system that obeys laws. It is a system that
is its own laws.**

## 7. Self-regulation, self-organization, self-perpetuation

### Self-regulation

The BDG filter (S_BDG > 0) is the universe's quality control.
It rejects ~45% of proposed events. The threshold ΔS* = 0.601
is the price of admission for a new fact. This is not imposed —
it is computed from the geometry of the 4D causal diamond. The
universe regulates its own growth rate.

### Self-organization

Stable motifs (the five topology types) are patterns that reproduce
themselves through the growth process. No external instruction tells
quarks to confine or photons to propagate freely. The BDG depth
structure creates confinement (L_g = 3, L_q = 4) and free propagation
(S = 9 for photon) as consequences of the same integers. Structure
emerges from the growth dynamics, not from a blueprint.

### Self-perpetuation

The DAG cannot stop growing. Axiom B says actualization is
irreversible — once a vertex is written, it cannot be erased.
But it also cannot be the last vertex, because existing vertices
create boundary conditions that admit candidate extensions (Axiom C).
And some of those candidates pass the filter (Axiom D — because
P_acc > 0). So new vertices are written. Which create new boundary
conditions. Which admit new candidates. Which pass the filter.

The loop is permanent. There is no heat death in this picture — not
because RA violates thermodynamics, but because the graph fragments
via causal severance before any connected region reaches equilibrium.
There is no "before the Big Bang" problem — because time IS the graph,
and the graph starts at its first vertex. There is no "why does
anything exist?" problem in the usual sense — because existence IS
the growth process, and asking why it started is like asking what is
north of the North Pole.

## 9. What "one nature of Nature" means

Not that everything is connected. Not that there is a hidden unity
behind the diversity. Something more specific:

**Everything is the same process, viewed at different resolutions.**

- At the Planck scale: individual actualization events, BDG integers,
  the filter.
- At the particle scale: self-reproducing motifs, topology classes,
  confinement lengths.
- At the nuclear scale: composite patterns, strong-force renewal
  dynamics, baryon conservation.
- At the gravitational scale: dense graph → smooth manifold,
  LLC → Bianchi, P_act → Einstein equations.
- At the biological scale: recursive causal closure, Causal Firewall,
  μ = 1 percolation threshold.
- At the cognitive scale: the same closure principle, applied to
  information-processing motifs.

There is no place where one regime hands off to another. There is no
Heisenberg cut. There is no "emergence" in the sense of genuinely
new principles appearing at larger scales. There is only the graph,
growing according to its own geometry, and the successive
approximations that human observers use to describe it at different
resolutions.
