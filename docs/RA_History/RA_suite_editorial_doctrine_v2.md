# RA Suite Editorial Doctrine v2

## Core stance
RA should be presented as a fundamentally different physical theory whose primitives are DAG growth, BDG filtering, local ledger conservation, and finitary actuality. Existing continuum theories remain important as comparison classes, but they are not RA's ontology, proof language, or mechanism.

## Governing rule
Every major claim should answer three questions:
1. What does RA say in its own native terms?
2. How does that compare with what GR/QFT/SM successfully describe?
3. What remains open, and what is the native path toward closure?

## Required compare-and-contrast pattern
Wherever relevant, the papers should explicitly distinguish:
- shared empirical domain: cases where RA aims to match established observations;
- different mechanism: cases where RA reaches similar observables by a different mathematical route;
- no analogue: cases where RA has no need for a standard-theory construct;
- open frontier: cases where RA does not yet have a closed native result.

## Style rules
- Do not write the papers as a history of RA's development.
- Do not write as if GR/QFT/SM are irrelevant to scientific comparison.
- Do not frame success as "recovering" standard theory structure unless the point is explicitly cartographic and secondary.
- Do explicitly compare predictions, domains of applicability, and mechanism differences.
- Do state clearly when RA currently lacks closure.
- Do state concrete native routes forward where they exist.

## Recommended recurring paragraph pattern
For each major section:
1. Native RA statement.
2. Observable target or phenomenon.
3. Comparison with standard theories:
   - same phenomenon;
   - different mechanism;
   - possible overlap in predictions;
   - limits of current RA closure.
4. Open problems and next native steps.

## Implications for specific recurring topics
- **Born rule**: discuss as a feature of the QM formalism, not as a primitive law RA must inherit.
- **GR comparisons**: discuss when RA targets the same observational regime, but emphasize causal-graph and severance mechanisms where these differ.
- **QFT comparisons**: discuss where RA may yield the same measurable regularities, but avoid importing virtual particles, renormalization machinery, gauge groups, or path-integral objects as active derivational content.
- **Causal severance**: present as a core RA strength with no close analogue in standard frameworks.
- **Unfinished sectors**: keep them explicit; do not hide them behind bridge language.

## Suite-level editorial aim
By the end of the suite, the reader should conclude:
- RA is not a reinterpretation of existing physics;
- RA is a discrete causal theory with its own mathematical engine;
- where RA already reaches known phenomena, it often does so by a materially different route;
- where RA is unfinished, the frontier is stated honestly and constructively.
