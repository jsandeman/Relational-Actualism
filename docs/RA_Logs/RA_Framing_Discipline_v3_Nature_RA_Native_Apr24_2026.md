
# RA Framing Discipline v3: Nature-Facing, RA-Native, Theory-Comparative

**Date:** April 24, 2026  
**Status:** Core framing principle for Relational Actualism. This document supersedes prior framing documents where they create ambiguity.

## 1. The central distinction

Relational Actualism (RA) must distinguish three layers that are often conflated:

1. **Nature itself** — observed quantities, structures, and regularities any successful physical theory must predict or explain.
2. **RA-native derivation** — the internal mechanisms, primitives, and mathematics by which RA attempts to predict or explain Nature.
3. **Legacy theoretical formalisms** — quantum mechanics, quantum field theory, the Standard Model, general relativity, ΛCDM, and other established frameworks that organize many of the same observations but do not define RA’s ontology or proof path.

RA must derive physics from its own primitives, but it must remain in open, intelligent comparison with the theories that currently organize the same empirical domains.

The governing rule is:

> **RA targets Nature, derives through RA primitives, and compares with existing theories without borrowing their ontology, mechanisms, or formal artifacts as goals.**

## 2. What RA is trying to derive

RA is not merely trying to produce a self-consistent graph theory. It is trying to derive, predict, or explain physical reality: the observed patterns in Nature from fundamental interactions to biological organization.

Examples of Nature-facing targets include:

- observed dimensionality;
- particle-like multiplicity and stability;
- measured rest masses;
- measured lifetime ratios;
- measured interaction strengths and ranges;
- scattering and decay patterns as observed experimental regularities;
- black-hole entropy and horizon phenomena;
- galaxy rotation curves;
- gravitational lensing maps;
- redshift–distance relations;
- CMB large-angle anomalies, including Axis-of-Evil-style alignments;
- structure formation and environmental cosmological signals;
- biological organization, persistence, and recursive closure;
- agency, cognition, and artificial-life-relevant organization.

These are not owned by any existing theory. They are Nature-facing targets.

## 3. What RA must not mistake for Nature

Many familiar objects are not Nature itself. They are artifacts, organizing constructs, or internal formalisms of particular theories.

Examples include:

- wavefunctions as ontological primitives;
- Hilbert space as a primitive arena;
- the Born rule as a primitive postulate;
- collapse rules;
- Lagrangians as fundamental objects;
- path integrals as fundamental mechanisms;
- gauge groups as primitive ontology;
- Standard Model quantum numbers as RA labels;
- renormalization-group flow as a physical process;
- running couplings as RA-native quantities;
- vacuum energy as primitive ontology;
- metric geometry as fundamental substrate;
- Friedmann equations as RA-native cosmology;
- Markov blankets as primitive biological boundaries;
- decoherence as the fundamental account of classicality.

These may be useful comparative descriptions. They are not RA’s targets.

- causal shielding and boundary turnover;
- RA-defined observables derived from the graph.

Where a result is numerical, the derivation must trace through RA-native structure or explicitly be labelled as comparative, hybrid, or provisional.

## 5. RA-native observables

Whenever possible, RA should define observables in its own terms before comparing them with measured data.

Preferred structure:

1. define an RA-native quantity;
2. derive or compute it from RA primitives;
3. identify the Nature-facing measurement it purports to predict or explain;
4. compare with measured values;
5. explicitly state whether the comparison is active support, derived native computation, comparative cartography, or open target.

Example pattern:

> RA defines a dimensionless interaction-strength observable from BDG arithmetic and motif renewal. Its value is \(1/\sqrt{72}\approx0.117851\). This is compared with measured strong-interaction strength summaries, without importing QCD running-coupling ontology as RA mechanism.

Bad pattern:

> RA derives the QCD running coupling.

The first targets Nature. The second targets a legacy formalism.

## 6. Comparison with existing theories is required

RA should not be written as if existing theories do not exist. Quantum mechanics, QFT, the Standard Model, GR, ΛCDM, systems biology, Assembly Theory, decoherence theory, and consciousness frameworks are important because they organize empirical domains RA also seeks to address.

Comparison is encouraged when it:

- orients the reader;
- identifies the empirical domain;
- clarifies what RA explains differently;
- shows where RA has no analogue of a familiar construct;
- distinguishes Nature-facing targets from formalism-specific artifacts;
- identifies what remains open.

Comparison is not allowed to become derivation.

## 7. “No analogue” is a strength when stated correctly

Because RA is a fundamentally different kind of physical theory, it should not be expected to contain analogues of every construct in continuum theories.

RA may have no direct analogue of:

- the Born rule as a primitive postulate;
- a wavefunction as ontology;
- gauge bosons as fundamental fields;
- Standard Model quantum numbers as primitive labels;
- spacetime metric as the base object;
- vacuum energy as a primitive substance;
- Markov blankets as primitive system boundaries.

This is not automatically a deficiency. The correct question is whether RA can predict or explain the corresponding Nature-facing observations.

Example:

- The Born rule is not Nature itself.
- Repeated experimental frequency data are Nature.
- RA need not derive the Born rule as such; RA must derive or explain the observed frequency regularities of repeated actualization experiments.

## 8. Status taxonomy

Every significant result should carry one of the following status labels.

### ANC — Active Native Compiled
A result formalized in a current compiled Lean file or equivalent formal corpus.

### DR — Derived Native
A result derived in RA-native mathematical language, but not necessarily formalized in Lean.

### CV — Computational Verification
A numerical result obtained by Python or equivalent computation from RA-native inputs.

### CC — Comparative Cartography
A comparison to a legacy-theory object or language. Useful for orientation; not active proof support.

### PI — Provisional Interpretation
A plausible interpretation or embedding whose mathematical derivation is incomplete.

### ONT — Open Native Target
A Nature-facing target RA must still derive, predict, or explain natively.

### ARCH — Archived / Non-active
A result from earlier project stages not presently used as active support.

## 9. Restoration rule for existing results

A result belongs in the papers if it satisfies the following test:

1. **Native path:** Does the mechanism trace to RA primitives or explicitly declared RA observables?
2. **Nature target:** Is the target a measured quantity, observed pattern, or real physical process?
3. **No illicit import:** Does the result avoid using legacy theory constructs as mechanism or primitive labels?
4. **Source traceability:** Is the result traceable to Lean, Python, derivation notes, or a clearly marked open programme?

If yes, restore it with an explicit status label and source pointer.

If partially yes, restore it only as comparative cartography, provisional interpretation, or open target.

If no, omit it or archive it.

## 10. Examples of correct classification

### Restore as active or derived support
- \(K=2/3\) as a Lean-verified arithmetic identity, with physical mass embedding marked separately.
- \(L_g=3\), \(L_q=4\) as Lean-supported finite motif closure lengths.
- \(\mu_{\mathrm{int}}=\exp(\sqrt{4\Delta S^*})\) as a derived native computational result.
- \(1/\sqrt{72}\) as an RA-native dimensionless interaction-strength scale, compared with measured strong-interaction summaries.
- kernel saturation identities \(D_{\mathrm{KL}}=\Delta S^*\) and \(TV=1-P_{\mathrm{acc}}\).
- antichain drift as computation-backed local graph statistic.

### Do not restore as active native support without rewriting
- sigma-filter classifications that use imported PDG quantum labels as RA categories;
- QCD running-coupling derivations as RA mechanism;
- Born-rule derivations written in wavefunction/Hilbert-space language;
- DFT/prebiotic computations inherited from legacy supplements unless migrated into the current support surface.

## 11. How papers should be written

The papers are not the theory’s working memory. They are public-facing projections of the theory.

Each paper should:

1. state RA-native mechanisms first;
2. name the Nature-facing targets;
3. compare with existing theories for orientation and contrast;
4. label the support status of important claims;
5. identify open native targets clearly;
6. avoid turning legacy formalism into the thing being derived.

## 12. How research should be organized

The project should maintain a structured results registry as the ground-truth map of the theory.

Every result should have:

- unique ID;
- statement;
- status;
- source files;
- dependencies;
- supported paper sections;
- Nature-facing target;
- caveats;
- next proof/computation task.

Papers, talks, summaries, and diagrams should be generated as views of this registry, not maintained as the only memory of the theory.

## 13. Final commitment

RA is a Nature-facing theory. It does not aim to reproduce the ontology of QM, QFT, SM, GR, or ΛCDM. It aims to explain the observed world by a different mechanism.

Therefore:

> **RA derives through DAG + BDG + LLC + finite actualization; targets measured Nature; compares intelligently with existing theories; and refuses to mistake legacy formal artifacts for physical reality itself.**
