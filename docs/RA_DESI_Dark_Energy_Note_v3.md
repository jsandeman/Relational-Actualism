An earlier version of this analysis attempted to derive the DESI

no density-dependent filtering at cosmic scales. The antichain
drift theorem, while proved, operates only during the first
~10⁻⁴³ seconds after nucleation when densities are Planck-scale.

**The corrected mechanism** does not require the BDG filter to act

This correction strengthens the programme: the result depends only

## 3. The Backreaction Mechanism

The real universe is not homogeneous. Cosmic voids occupy ~60–70%
of the volume at z = 0 (Pan et al. 2012, Cautun et al. 2014).

In a Λ = 0 universe, voids expand as Milne, matter regions as EdS.
A photon traversing this patchwork accumulates a redshift history
that differs from any single Friedmann model. When fit with
homogeneous ΛCDM, the fitter requires Ω_Λ > 0 — even though
Λ = 0 in the underlying physics.

This is the cosmological backreaction effect, studied by Buchert
(2000, 2008), Wiltshire (2007, 2009), and Räsänen (2004). Two
questions have limited the programme:

**(Q1) Why should Λ = 0?** Standard QFT predicts Λ ~ 10¹²⁰ in
Planck units. Without a mechanism for Λ = 0, the backreaction
programme is an assumption, not a derivation.

**(Q2) Is the effect large enough?** Green & Wald (2014) argue
backreaction is perturbatively small. Wiltshire (2009) and Buchert
et al. (2015) argue it can be significant when properly averaged.

**RA resolves Q1:** Λ = 0 is derived from P_act. This converts the
backreaction programme from "assume Λ = 0 and see what happens" to
"derive Λ = 0 from actualization physics and compute the
consequences." The Milne shape result (Ω_Λ(apparent) = 0.68)
provides concrete quantitative support for Q2.

**Current status of the w₀, wₐ fit:** The pure Milne limit gives

2009), which performs the proper averaging for a Λ = 0 universe,
obtains w₀ ≈ -0.8, wₐ ≈ -0.2 — in the right quadrant though
not an exact match to DESI.

**Honest assessment:** RA has shape-level evidence (Ω_Λ = 0.68),
not yet a parameter-level fit (w₀, wₐ). The specific values
require the Buchert formalism applied to RA, identified as the

## 5. Distinctive Environmental Predictions

The strongest claims separating RA from both ΛCDM and quintessence
are not about the global w(z) — many models predict evolving w —
but about **environmental structure** in the expansion signal.

**(T1) BAO scale differs between void and filament subsamples.**
At fixed redshift, the BAO standard ruler should appear larger
inside cosmic voids (more Milne-like expansion) than inside
filaments (more EdS-like). ΛCDM predicts no environment-dependent
BAO splitting at fixed z. This is testable with DESI DR1/DR2 by
measuring BAO in void vs filament subsamples independently.

**(T2) Apparent w(z) correlates with void volume fraction.**
The apparent equation of state at each redshift should track the
void volume fraction f_v(z) at that redshift. This correlation
is a structural consequence of the backreaction mechanism:
- ΛCDM: w = -1 always (no correlation with anything)
- Quintessence: w(z) from field dynamics (no void correlation)
- RA: w(z) must correlate with f_v(z) because the expansion
  mechanism is density-dependent GR with Λ = 0

This is the sharpest near-term test. DESI measures both BAO
(expansion history) and the 3D galaxy distribution (void
statistics) from the same survey volume.

**(T3) SN Hubble residuals correlate with local environment.**
Type Ia supernovae in void environments should appear to recede
faster than the global average; those in filaments, slower. This
is partially observed in the "local flow" literature but has not
been tested as a structural prediction of Λ = 0 cosmology.

**(T4) The Hubble tension is a density gradient effect.**
The locally-measured H₀ ≈ 73 km/s/Mpc exceeds the CMB-inferred
H₀ ≈ 67 km/s/Mpc because local measurements preferentially
sample low-density lines of sight near the KBC void. RA
parameter-free prediction: H_local = 73.6 km/s/Mpc.

**(T5) No fundamental dark energy field exists.**
Future precision surveys (Euclid, Rubin, Roman) should find that
the apparent w(z) is entirely explained by the void fraction
evolution — with no residual requiring a new field or constant.
